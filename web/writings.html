Do What you love

STORYTELLING IS, AT ITS VERY CORE, A CRAFT. It’s more of an art, the science people can question this :). This is more than a story, it’s a journey I took without even knowing. The first quarter of 2018 was a very awesome and thrilling period of my life. I just got this huge role with an amazing FMCG company which meant a lot to me at that time.

For a young chap at that time, just trying to find his feet, I tell you, this was huge. It involved me changing my location, which I was so happy to do. I had just one thing on my mind back then which was “growing through the ranks in the cooperate ladder”. But something dramatic happened, I birth something new. Two weeks on my new role, I just fell out of love with it, it all became an anticlimax.

I had a newfound love, career-wise, and it was Software Engineering, so I took the deep dive and changed my route. No one earlier coached me on “tips for a career change”, so I struggled initially. I was this consistent young boy :) coding every day without a mentor. I later resigned a year and four months after and decided to get into the tech scene wholeheartedly, and since then I haven’t looked back.

My advice to young people who are thinking of having a career change is that they should strengthen their transferable skills portfolio, yea it does come in handy. Just do what you love and be unapologetic.

A deep truth

The void of being left alone is what no one should experience, the seemingly possible event that can happen to one in that state is very tragic. The pain stained on the mind is so hard to let go, thus depression sets in. You are forced into a valley of indescribable thoughts, then you start to ponder, you wonder how the power of emptiness ate deep right through you, this is a story of a young boy who was left alone in the dark with nothing but a mark of rejection.

This crisis is abominable, the chaos so unimaginable. Looking for who will draw out this pain he was raged, so he sorts help from a sage, the one in which everyone talked about. The sage asked ‘do you have an ally?’ he said ‘no, I am all alone’, the sage soliloquizes ‘little wonder’.

He was asked to come back in 3 days, but that was the last of the young boy, his mind now wonders with an unlikely ally called depression. He left a note on his last day, the deep truth of a past he thought could go away.
